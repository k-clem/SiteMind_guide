Step 3A
The walk-in pitch

Walk in relaxed. You're doing them a favor. Find the owner or manager — not just an employee at the counter.

Opening — say this first
"Hey, my name is [your name] — I work with a web design company called SiteMind. We noticed your business doesn't have a website yet, and we actually put together a sneak peek of what one could look like for you. It's exactly what the final site would look like. Would you mind taking a look?"

Show them the mock on your phone. Give them 20–30 seconds. Don't say anything — let them look.

If they seem interested
"This is exactly what it would look like live online — your business name, your services, your phone number, everything already in there. We can have it fully live within a couple of days. It's a one-time price, no monthly fees, no subscriptions. Starts at $400 for a clean single page."
Closing — ask for the sale
"Want to move forward with it? All I need is half upfront — $[X] — and then the other half when your site goes live. I can send you the payment info right now, and you'll have your website up within a couple of days."

Step 4
Handling objections

Most hesitation is just habit. Stay calm, don't argue, and use these responses.

"We don't really need a website."
Say: "A lot of your customers search online before they come in — if they can't find you, they go to a competitor. A website puts you on the map 24/7, even when you're closed."
"How much does it cost?"
Say: "It's a one-time price — $400 for a clean single-page site, $500 for a full site with all your info. That's it, no monthly fees after."
"I need to think about it."
Say: "Totally fair — can I leave you my number and send you the link so you can show it to anyone else who needs to see it? No pressure at all."
"We already have someone working on it."
Say: "No problem at all! If it ever falls through, keep our number — we can turn it around in a couple of days."
"How do I know it'll actually look like that?"
Say: "You're literally looking at the finished product right now. What you see on your screen is exactly what goes live. It's already built — we just need your go-ahead."
Step 5

Never leave without the deposit. If they say yes and you walk out empty-handed, it's likely a lost deal.

3
Collect the remaining 50% on delivery
When the site goes live, you send the client the link and collect the balance. Then both you and Kevan get paid.
